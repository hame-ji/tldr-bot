## Item 2

URL: https://youtu.be/XVNH8MPRgVY?si=GoG_Yd06VpNJjpi8

Title: AI, Jevons Paradox, and Workforce Demand

TL;DR: Applying Jevons Paradox to AI suggests that as automation lowers task costs and increases efficiency, overall demand for human labor and advanced skills will actually increase.

Key points:
- Forward-thinking organizations view AI as augmented intelligence that enables new capabilities rather than just a tool for headcount reduction.
- Jevons Paradox dictates that as technological efficiency improves and costs drop, the total demand for the underlying resource increases due to new, previously impractical use cases.
- AI will expand the total volume of work, requiring employees to develop adaptability, lifelong learning habits, and strong critical thinking.
- Human oversight remains mandatory because AI systems can make errors or generate outputs that misalign with intended goals.

Why it matters:
- Organizations focusing solely on using AI to cut costs risk falling behind competitors who leverage the technology to innovate, scale operations, and explore new business opportunities.

Evidence:
- In 1865, William Stanley Jevons observed that more efficient steam engines led to increased total coal consumption in England instead of decreasing it.
- The historical introduction of spreadsheet software automated basic arithmetic but caused the overall demand for human financial analysis to explode.

Caveat:
- The source material is produced by IBM Technology, an enterprise AI vendor, which may introduce bias toward optimistic workforce narratives regarding AI adoption and job creation.
