Title: Linear's Shift from Issue Tracking to AI-Driven Execution

TL;DR: As traditional issue tracking becomes bottlenecked by process overhead, Linear is pivoting toward an AI-agent-driven system where context and rapid prototyping replace rigid handoffs and complex specifications.

Key points:
- Traditional issue trackers focus heavily on rigid handoffs and process management, which creates significant administrative overhead and slows down engineering teams.
- Linear is moving away from standard sprint planning toward context-driven execution, leveraging integrations where customer feedback and requirements directly guide AI agents.
- Building rapid, functioning prototypes is presented as a superior planning method compared to writing extensive specifications, as it quickly reveals technical constraints and edge cases.
- The current trend of assigning specific human corporate roles to AI tools is likely a transitional phase, with workflows ultimately returning to raw code generation as the primary planning mechanism.

Why it matters:
- This evolution in project management allows software teams to spend less time managing procedural tickets and more time focusing on product intent, judgment, and actual implementation.

Evidence:
- Linear reported that over a three-month period, the volume of work completed by agents grew 5x, with agents authoring nearly 25% of all new issues.
- At Twitch, the speaker successfully replaced the process of debating 20-page specifications with building 1-to-3-day working prototypes to identify technical shortcomings early.

Caveat:
- The speaker acknowledges that because AI models are trained on historical human data, they might actually perform more reliably when mimicking traditional human workflows rather than immediately adopting pure code-as-planning methods.
