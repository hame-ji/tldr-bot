Title: Outcome-Oriented Design in the AI Era

TL;DR: Generative AI is shifting digital design from manual, step-by-step interaction to intent-based outcome specification.

Key points:
- Generative AI systems allow users to describe their desired results rather than instructing computers on how to achieve them.
- Outcome-oriented design focuses heavily on final user goals while strategically automating standard aspects of interface design.
- The role of the designer is shifting from crafting a single, rigid user experience to defining adaptable frameworks and system boundaries.
- AI leverages these foundational frameworks to dynamically generate individualized paths based on specific user needs.

Why it matters:
- This paradigm change forces user experience professionals to evolve from direct interface builders into "architects of possibilities" who orchestrate broader system interactions.

Evidence:
- The transition is illustrated by vacation planning, comparing the traditional manual coordination of flights and hotels across multiple websites to AI systems that automate booking based on stated user goals.

Caveat:
- The source relies entirely on conceptual metaphors and high-level theory, omitting empirical data or quantitative metrics regarding the implementation of outcome-oriented design.
