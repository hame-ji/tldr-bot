**Title:** Generating Code from Specifications: A Critical Analysis

**Key Points:**
- Specification documents often resemble code, making it hard for agents to produce reliable code.
- Agentic coding advocates claim to bypass manual coding, but their approach is misleading.
- Misconception 1: Specs are simpler than code; they force agents to treat specs as code.
- Misconception 2: Specs require more thought than coding, which is unrealistic given current industry pressures.
- Real-world examples (e.g., Symphony project) show specification documents can be verbose and poorly structured.
- Specs should not be seen as substitutes for code; they are tools that demand careful, deliberate engineering.

**Why It Matters:**  
Understanding these misconceptions is crucial for engineers to recognize the limitations of agentic coding and ensure that specifications are clear, maintainable, and genuinely reflective of intended functionality.
