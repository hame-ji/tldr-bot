Title: Anthropic's Mythos Preview Model Withheld Due to Exploit Capabilities

TL;DR: Anthropic has developed a new AI model called Mythos Preview that demonstrates advanced autonomous capabilities to find and exploit zero-day vulnerabilities, leading the company to restrict its public release.

Key points:
- Anthropic's Mythos Preview scored 77.8% on the SWE-bench Pro benchmark, outperforming the previous Opus 46 model by over 20%.
- The model autonomously identifies and explores zero-day vulnerabilities across major operating systems and web browsers when directed.
- Anthropic is withholding public access to Mythos Preview, opting instead to build new safeguards into an upcoming, less risky model.
- Access to the Mythos Preview model will be heavily restricted to select major technology companies and the US government.

Why it matters:
- AI tools are transitioning from generating overwhelming volumes of false security reports to autonomously discovering and exploiting legitimate, deeply hidden vulnerabilities in critical software infrastructure.

Evidence:
- The model autonomously chained four vulnerabilities to write a complex JIT heap spray that escaped both renderer and OS sandboxes.
- It autonomously developed a remote execution code exploit on a FreeBSD NFS server, granting full root access by splitting a 20-gadget ROP chain over multiple packets.

Caveat:
- Anthropic's "too dangerous to release" messaging heavily mirrors past AI marketing hype cycles, raising skepticism about whether the claims are entirely genuine or partially promotional.
