Title: State of AI Developer Models, Harnesses, and Subscriptions

TL;DR: A March 2026 evaluation of AI developer tools identifies GPT 5.4 as the most capable coding model and Cursor as the highest-value subscription tier.

Key points:
- GPT 5.4 dominates in instruction following and complex problem solving, but Opus 46 is strictly required for front-end tasks due to GPT 5.4's severe regressions in UI generation.
- GPT 5.4 Mini and Composer 2.0 serve as highly effective, fast sleeper picks for sub-agency tasks and specific file edits, though Composer is locked to the Cursor ecosystem.
- Cursor remains the premier primary editor because of its top-tier tab completion and cloud agent sandboxes, despite ongoing stability and performance issues.
- T3 Code offers a highly performant, minimal harness alternative for users frustrated by the heavy resource drain of the default Codex desktop application.
- Model-agnostic subscriptions provide the best value, with the $200/month Cursor tier offering access to GPT, Claude, Gemini, and open-source models without restrictive vendor lock-in.

Why it matters:
- Strategically pairing the right coding models with flexible harnesses prevents developers from overspending on redundant $200/month subscriptions while maximizing autonomous agent capabilities.

Evidence:
- The $200/month Cursor subscription yields an estimated $400 to $500 worth of flexible API credits.
- The author currently processes nearly a billion tokens a week through the $200/month Codex subscription without hitting its double rate limits.

Caveat:
- The tooling landscape is highly volatile, making these recommendations specific to March 2026, and the author explicitly discloses a professional bias regarding their top-ranked T3 Code harness.
