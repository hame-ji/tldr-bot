Summary: A review of study techniques for better learning.

Key points:
- Effective methods improve learning and retention.
- Self-testing, distributed practice, and elaborative interrogation are most useful.
- Rereading and highlighting are less effective.
- Interleaved practice and spaced repetition boost long-term memory.
- Techniques like summarization and keyword mnemonics have mixed results.

Why it matters:
- Helps students and teachers choose proven strategies for better performance.

What matters:
- Focus on techniques backed by research.
- Use methods that fit different learning needs and contexts.

Caveat:
- Some methods lack strong evidence or are time-consuming.

TL;DR: Prioritize proven study techniques for better learning outcomes.
