Title: The End of AI Inference Subsidization

TL;DR: Major AI providers are phasing out heavily subsidized access to frontier models as the computing costs of complex tasks outpace the benefits of user acquisition.

Key points:
- AI inference costs are climbing despite cheaper model releases because users are generating exponentially more tokens through multi-step tasks and agent workflows.
- Advertising and data collection are mathematically insufficient to cover the high infrastructure costs associated with processing complex free user prompts.
- Tech giants originally subsidized inference to steal market share, aiming to attract users who would eventually convert to paid enterprise subscriptions.
- Google is restricting free access to Gemini Pro because its generous tier attracted a massive base of non-converting users that depleted GPU capacity for paying customers.
- Providers are locking down their ecosystems to restrict third-party clients, ensuring heavily subsidized users remain locked-in to eventually recoup costs when compute gets cheaper.

Why it matters:
- Developers relying on free or massively discounted AI compute must prepare for strict rate limits and transition to sustainable, paid usage models.

Evidence:
- A massive cost variance exists in real-world usage, where complex prompts can require up to 400 times more tokens than simple ones, sometimes costing over $1 per individual message.
- High-tier $200-per-month subscriptions currently provide up to $5,000 in underlying computing value, an unsustainable 25x subsidization rate.

Caveat:
- The speaker operates an independent AI tool (T3 Chat/Code) that pays standard API rates, directly influencing their critical stance on the market impact of big-tech subsidies.
