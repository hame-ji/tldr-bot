1) Title: Common Problems Undermining Product Discovery

2) Key Points:
- **Must-Have Features**: A two-track system where some features bypass discovery, labeled as "must-haves" based on opinion rather than evidence. This starves discovery of resources and reduces innovation.
- **Weak Goals**: Teams lack clear, measurable outcomes, leading to an output-focused "feature factory" mindset. Without strong goals, idea evaluation and experimentation are ineffective.
- **Solutions for Must-Haves**: Demonstrate discovery's business value (reducing waste, shortening time-to-outcome), build trust by sharing evidence, and consider a gradual transition using hybrid roadmaps.
- **Solutions for Weak Goals**: Start within the product org by building multi-level business models (e.g., dual metrics trees) and using OKRs correctly to align on outcomes.
- **Systemic Roots**: Both problems stem from traditional operating models—leadership's lack of strategic context, output focus, and internal misalignment.

3) Why it Matters:
Effective product discovery is critical for building products that deliver user value and business results. These systemic issues cause companies to waste resources on unvalidated ideas, ship features that don't move key metrics, and lose agility and competitiveness. Addressing them is essential to shift from opinion-based delivery to evidence-guided development.
