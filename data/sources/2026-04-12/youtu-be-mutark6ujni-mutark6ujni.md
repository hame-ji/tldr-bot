Title: Exponential Growth in AI Agent Autonomy

TL;DR: Independent evaluations reveal that the duration AI can autonomously execute technical tasks is doubling every four months, accelerating toward a critical threshold of recursive self-improvement.

Key points:
- METR evaluates AI capabilities based on the "horizon of autonomy," measuring how long an AI can perform complex, self-correcting technical work without human intervention.
- The progression of autonomous capabilities follows an exponential curve, with the doubling time for task endurance recently accelerating from seven months to four months.
- Industry leaders who previously anticipated a slow, gradual capability increase now expect a rapid, explosive "fast takeoff" in AI development.
- A critical threshold will be crossed when AI systems achieve enough autonomy to assist in machine learning research, creating a feedback loop where AI builds subsequent models faster than human comprehension.

Why it matters:
- Engineering workflows will fundamentally change as the industry transitions from prompt-based oracles to autonomous agents capable of executing complex, multi-day technical tasks without supervision.

Evidence:
- METR data tracks autonomous task duration scaling from seconds with GPT-3.5 in 2022, to roughly 1 hour with Claude 3.7 Sonnet in early 2025, and approaching 5 hours with Claude Opus 4.5 by late 2025.
- A February 2026 METR update analyzing Claude Opus 4 confirmed the autonomy doubling rate compressed to every four months.

Caveat:
- The source heavily frames its data around a call to action for political regulation, utilizing alarmist comparisons to the early COVID-19 pandemic to encourage viewers to lobby their representatives.
