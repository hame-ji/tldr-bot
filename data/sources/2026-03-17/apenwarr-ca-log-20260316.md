1) Title: Every Layer of Review Makes You 10x Slower

2) Key Points:
- Each additional approval layer in a development process increases wall-clock time by roughly 10x, primarily due to waiting, not effort.
- AI-generated code accelerates the initial coding step but does not address the bottleneck of subsequent human review stages, which remain slow.
- Traditional multi-stage Quality Assurance (QA) can paradoxically reduce overall quality by creating misaligned incentives and obscuring root causes.
- The solution proposed is inspired by Deming's quality philosophy: eliminate layers of review by building trust and engineering quality into the system from the start, enabling teams to "stop the line" for defects.
- Smaller, trusted teams with well-defined interfaces and the ability to rapidly iterate (aided by AI) may be better positioned to achieve high quality without heavy review overhead.

3) Why it matters:
This analysis highlights a critical inefficiency in software development processes, especially as AI tools increase code output. It argues that simply adding more AI-generated code or review stages will not improve speed or quality. Instead, organizations must rethink their review culture and team structures—focusing on trust, modularity, and systemic quality—to avoid becoming slower and more error-prone despite technological advances.
