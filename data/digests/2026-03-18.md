## Item 1

URL: https://siddhantkhare.com/writing/ai-fatigue-is-real

**Title:** AI Fatigue: The Hidden Cost of AI-Augmented Engineering

**Key Points:**
1.  **The Productivity Paradox:** AI makes individual tasks faster, but this often leads to an increased volume of tasks and constant context-switching, raising the overall cognitive load and coordination cost for the human engineer.
2.  **Shift from Creator to Reviewer:** The engineer's role evolves from writing code to evaluating, judging, and correcting AI-generated output, which is a fundamentally more draining and less "flow-state" inducing activity.
3.  **Nondeterminism and Trust:** AI output is probabilistic, not deterministic. This breaks the engineer's contract of predictable systems, requiring constant vigilance and creating a low-grade, persistent stress.
4.  **Tool Churn and FOMO:** The rapid pace of new AI tools, frameworks, and models creates a "FOMO treadmill," leading to wasted time on migrations and shallow expertise, rather than deep mastery.
5.  **Cognitive Atrophy and New Traps:** Over-reliance on AI can atrophy core engineering thinking skills. New failure modes emerge, like the "prompt spiral" (endlessly refining prompts) and perfectionism clashing with AI's "good enough" output.
6.  **Sustainable Strategies:** The author advocates for intentional boundaries: time-boxing AI use, separating thinking time from AI-assisted execution, accepting ~70% usable output from AI, and focusing review on critical areas (security, data handling).

**Why it Matters:**
This article highlights a critical, under-discussed human factor in AI adoption. For software engineers, it validates the feeling of increased exhaustion despite higher output and provides a framework for understanding the root causes. It argues that sustainable productivity requires managing cognitive load and establishing boundaries, not just adopting more tools. The insights are crucial for individual engineers to avoid burnout and for teams/organizations to design humane, effective AI-augmented workflows.

**Core Thesis**
*   **Becoming an engineering manager is a highly valuable career move**, not for the title or pay, but because it forces developers to build universal communication and goal-setting skills that are essential for the future of AI-driven "agentic" engineering [1, 2]. 

**Key Supporting Points and Evidence**
*   **Management requires sacrificing personal freedom** and autonomy to focus entirely on setting the team up for success, rather than choosing how or when you do your own work [3].
*   **The traditional management ladder is flattening** and highly competitive, making it increasingly difficult to climb from a standard engineering manager (EM) to a Director or VP [4].
*   **Individual Contributors (ICs) often have higher earning potential.** Senior or Staff IC roles often pay more across the industry than EM roles, and hiring ICs carries less risk for companies than hiring managers [5].
*   **Managing humans translates directly to managing AI.** Learning to communicate clearly and concisely with human engineers directly improves your ability to prompt, evaluate, and direct AI agents [1, 6].

**Notable Examples, Numbers, and Concrete Details**
*   **Corporate flattening:** Amazon recently increased its IC-to-manager ratio by 15%, meaning managers are overseeing more people and fewer management roles are available [4].
*   **Compensation differences:** A developer choosing to become a Staff Engineer and switch companies could make **20% to 30% more money** than accepting an internal EM promotion [7].
*   **The Stockfish AI test:** When the speaker asked an AI to write code to beat the Stockfish 17 chess engine, the AI failed because the prompt dictated narrow instructions rather than the actual goal [8, 9]. Once the true goal ("build a chess engine from scratch") was communicated, the AI succeeded, mirroring how managers must lead human teams instead of micromanaging them [6, 9, 10].
*   **The counteroffer rule:** Accepting a counteroffer from your current company after committing to a new job permanently destroys trust and will likely get you blacklisted by the new employer [11].

**Actionable Takeaways and Decisions to Make**
*   **Accept management opportunities:** Use the EM role specifically as a training ground to dramatically improve your communication, clarity, and ability to build alignment [2, 12].
*   **Communicate goals, not steps:** Whether directing human developers or prompting AI agents, clearly define the ultimate objective (the "why") rather than micromanaging the execution (the "how") [6, 9, 13].
*   **Prioritize universal skills:** Because the exact future of the tech industry is unpredictable, focus on developing communication and leadership skills over obsessing over specific job titles or ladders [2, 14].
